# TO-BE Process Architecture

## Overview

This diagram represents the automated technology cost allocation process implemented through Microsoft Excel and VBA.

The solution replaced multiple manual activities with a standardized workflow capable of importing inventory information, assigning costs automatically, consolidating data, and generating financial validation reports.

# Process Description

## Step 1 – Invoice Reception

Technology service invoices are received from suppliers.

---

## Step 2 – Inventory Import

Technology inventory information is imported into dedicated worksheets corresponding to each service category.

Examples:

* Advance
* Encryption
* COVE
* RMM
* Email

---

## Step 3 – User and Cost Center Mapping

The automation matches:

* Technology assets
* Assigned users
* Organizational cost centers

This removes the need for manual matching activities.

---

## Step 4 – Automated Allocation Engine

The VBA allocation logic:

* Calculates allocation percentages.
* Assigns costs automatically.
* Generates proportional distributions.
* Reduces calculation errors.

---

## Step 5 – Data Quality Controls

The solution applies standardization controls including:

* Text normalization.
* Character normalization.
* Consistent matching rules.

These controls improve allocation accuracy.

---

## Step 6 – Consolidation Layer

Information from all technology services is consolidated into a centralized worksheet.

This creates a single source of information for financial review.

---

## Step 7 – Prefactura Generation

The solution automatically generates detailed allocation outputs including:

* Cost center assignments.
* Allocation percentages.
* Assigned values.
* Validation support reports.

---

## Step 8 – Executive Summary Reporting

A consolidated summary is generated to provide visibility of technology costs by cost center.

This supports:

* Financial governance.
* Budget tracking.
* Management reporting.
